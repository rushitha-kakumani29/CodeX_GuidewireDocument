"#Problem Statement:"
            The growing digital space in India heavily relies on the services of gig-based delivery staff who work on prominent digital platforms like Zomato, Swiggy, Zepto, Amazon, and Dunzo. The gig-based delivery staff are instrumental in delivering timely services to millions of consumers on a daily basis. Unlike regular salaried employees, the income of gig-based delivery staff is heavily dependent on the weekly or daily income they earn from these platforms. The income of gig-based delivery staff is highly vulnerable to any external factors that may impact their ability to earn a living. However, the situation is that the delivery staff may encounter situations that are beyond their control. Situations like extreme heatwaves, heavy rainfall, flooding, extreme levels of air pollution, sudden curfews, strikes, or closure of markets may impact the delivery staff's ability to work. In such situations, the platforms may suspend services or the delivery staff may be unable to work due to the prevailing conditions. This may result in the loss of 20 to 30% of the income for the gig-based delivery staff.


"#Solution:"
  The solution that is being proposed is an AI-based parametric insurance platform, which will assist gig delivery workers in coping with losses in their income due to factors such as severe weather conditions, pollution, floods, and curfews, among others.

1. User Registration and Authentication
      The gig workers will have to register themselves as users on the platform. During registration, they will have to provide the following information:
      Unique Platform ID like Delivery Partner ID from organizations like Amazon, Rapido, Zomato, etc.
      Registered Phone Number , which will be the same as in the delivery platform for payouts
      Bank Account Information, which will be the same as in the delivery platform for payouts
      In order to ensure an additional layer of security, biometric authentication like fingerprint recognition or face recognition may be used for user login and           other critical transactions on the platform.
2. Income Period Selection
      The worker is requested to select the income period during the setup process. The income period selection options are as follows:
          Weekly
          Biweekly
          Monthly
      This is to enable the system to map the insurance premium deduction with the income pattern of the worker.
   a. Weekly Income Plan
       If the worker chooses the weekly income option:
       If income is credited from the delivery platform → 10% of the credited income will be deducted as the insurance premium.
       If income is not credited during that week → 5% will be deducted from the current bank balance of the user for coverage.
     Example:
       Weekly Income Credited = ₹5000
       Premium Deducted (10%) = ₹500
       Total Amount deducted from a month = 500 * 4 = 2000
     If no income is credited:
       Premium Deducted = 5% from available balance
   b. Biweekly Income Plan
       If the worker chooses the biweekly income option:
       If income is credited from the delivery platform -> 10% of the credited income will be deducted as the insurance premium.
       If income is not credited during that week → 5% will be deducted from the current bank balance of the user for coverage.
     Example:
       Biweekly Income = ₹10,000
       Premium Deducted (10%) = ₹1000
       Total Amount deducted from a Month = 1000 * 2 = 2000
     If no income is credited:
       Premium Deducted = 5% from available balance
   C. Monthly Income Plan
        If the worker chooses the biweekly income option:
       If income is credited from the delivery platform -> 10% of the credited income will be deducted as the insurance premium.
       If income is not credited during that week → 5% will be deducted from the current bank balance of the user for coverage.
     Example:
       Monthly Income = ₹40,000
       Premium Deducted (10%) = ₹4000
     If no income is credited:
       Premium Deducted = 5% from available balance
   
3. Minimum Balance Requirement
      The user needs to ensure that the minimum balance is maintained to keep the insurance active. The minimum balance is ₹1000.
      If the balance is reduced to an amount lower than the minimum balance:
      Insurance coverage may be temporarily suspended.

4. Worker Exit / Resignation Policy
      If the gig worker wants to exit or resign from the delivery platform:
         1. The insurance period will be calculated based on the registration date up to the resignation date.
         2. The amount of Rs. 50 will be deducted as administrative charges per week.
         3. The remaining amount will be refunded back to the user.
         4. The insurance account will be disabled for the user.
5. Disaster Detection and Compensation
      The system constantly tracks environmental factors through external sources of information and AI models.
      Some of the events that are monitored:
        Extreme heat waves
        Heavy rainfalls
        Flood alerts
        Severe air pollution
        Curfews imposed by the government
      AI models track environmental factors and check whether the thresholds are breached or not.
